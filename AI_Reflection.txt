AI Reflections
==============

I used AI as a tool, not a replacement for thinking. Decisions were mine and AI handled the execution.

For the database design I studied the raw API data myself and mapped out the relationships before involving AI. 
I then described the schema and had it generate the ERD. 
Reviewing the output was where I caught redundant fields like stored goal counts and the score by period table, both of which I flagged as derivable from goal events. 
The winner field was also my call to store as a foreign key rather than a plain string.

For implementation I worked in Cursor. 
My workflow was to decide what I wanted, describe it clearly, let AI write the code, read through everything, and then test the endpoints. 
The review step was always necessary. 
The main value AI provided was speed on boilerplate, which let me focus on the parts that actually required decisions.
